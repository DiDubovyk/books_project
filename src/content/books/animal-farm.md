_Animal Farm_ is a short allegorical novella by George Orwell, published in 1945. Set on a fictional English farm, the story follows a group of animals who overthrow their human farmer in the hope of building a fairer, equal society — only to find that power corrupts those who seize it.

## The Story

The rebellion is sparked by Old Major, a wise old pig who shares his vision of a world free from human oppression. After his death, the pigs — led by the clever and ambitious Napoleon and the idealistic Snowball — organize the animals and successfully drive out the farmer, Mr. Jones.

They establish _Animalism_, a philosophy built on seven commandments, the most important being: **"All animals are equal."** However, as time passes, the pigs gradually consolidate power, rewrite the rules, and begin to mirror the very humans they replaced.

## Why It Matters

Orwell wrote _Animal Farm_ as a direct allegory of the Soviet Union under Stalin, but its message extends far beyond that. It is a timeless warning about:

- How revolutionary ideals can be corrupted by those in power
- The danger of propaganda and the manipulation of language
- The importance of critical thinking and questioning authority

## Memorable Quote

> "All animals are equal, but some animals are more equal than others."

## Verdict

A masterpiece of political satire that can be read in a single sitting yet resonates for a lifetime. Essential reading for anyone interested in history, politics, or the nature of power.
